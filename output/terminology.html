Terminology

Vaasudev Narayanan - Fri 03 December 2010 -

A tennis match comprises sets; sets comprise games; games comprise points. A point is won when two players (Singles matches) contest a rally.

A rally consists of a sequence of back and forth shots. The opening shot of the rally is the serve.

A frame in the rally is called an action frame if any player is in direct contact with ball via a racquet in that frame.

Our dataset consists of sequences of such action frames. Every second frame belongs to the same player. For example, consider a rally F1F2F3F4F5 and players P1 and P2.

If P1 is serving, then F1 corresponds to P1 in contact with the ball, F2 corresponds to P2, F3 corresponds to P3 and so on. In the last frame (F5 here).
